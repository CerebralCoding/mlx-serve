mlx-serve/Welcome · Getting started

You installed MLX Core.
Here's the fun part.

It's a real AI assistant that lives entirely on your Mac — so it's private, free forever, and works offline. Chat with it, ask it about your own files, make images, music and voices, and let it run errands on your computer. Here's how to start in five minutes.

Start here

Up and running in three steps

No terminal, no setup, no keys to copy. If you can install an app, you can do this.

1

Find it in your menu bar

MLX Core doesn't open a big window on its own — it lives in the menu bar at the top-right of your screen. Click its icon and choose Open Chat.

2

Download a model

Open Models and pick the recommended one — it's sized to your Mac. Click Download and wait a minute. This happens once; after that it runs offline.

3

Start chatting

Type a question and press return. Or, from any app, press ⌃Space to summon a quick prompt box. That's it — you're talking to your own private AI.

A "model" is the AI's brain — a file you download once. Bigger models are smarter but slower; the recommended one is a great place to start, and you can grab others any time.

Try these first

Three things that feel like magic

The fastest way to "get" it. Each one takes under a minute and shows what only a local AI can do.

One · ⌃Space

Ask anything, from anywhere

Press ⌃Space over any app — your browser, an email, a game — and a little prompt box appears. Ask your question; the answer streams in right there. No tab-switching, no logging in.

It's the fastest note-to-self, quick-question, "what's the word for…" habit you'll pick up. Follow-ups remember the conversation, and Esc tucks it away.

Two · Your files

Chat with your own documents

Drag a folder into the chat and ask about what's inside — a lease, a stack of PDFs, your class notes, a pile of receipts. "Which invoice is unpaid?" · "Summarize this into five bullets." · "Quiz me on chapter 3."

Because nothing leaves your Mac, this is the stuff you'd never paste into an online chatbot — taxes, medical letters, contracts — answered safely at home.

Three · Voice

Just talk to it, hands-free

Turn on Voice Mode from the menu bar, say "Hey Loki," and speak. It listens, thinks, and answers out loud — perfect while cooking, tidying, or away from the keyboard. Your voice is transcribed on the Mac and never uploaded.

Want it to reply in a familiar voice? Record a short clip and it can even answer in a cloned voice.

It can actually do things

An assistant with hands

Flip on Agent mode and it can tidy files, look things up, and build small things for you — with a safety rail you control.

Agent mode

Give it a chore, watch it work

Ask in plain words: "Organize my Downloads folder by type." · "Rename these photos by the date they were taken." · "Build me a little birthday-countdown web page" — and it opens it in your browser when it's done.

  • You set the leash. Choose how much it's allowed to do; anything riskier pauses and asks first.
  • It stays in its lane. File actions are confined to a folder you pick.
  • Nothing to configure. The tools are built in — just describe the goal.

…and it can keep working while you don't

FAQ

The questions everyone asks first

Is it really free?

Yes — completely. It's open source under the MIT license: no subscription, no accounts, no per-message cost. You download a model once and use it as much as you like. Even the images, music, voice, video, and 3D are free to make.

Is my data private?

Yes. Everything runs on your Mac — no telemetry, no analytics, no cloud service in the loop. You can turn Wi-Fi off and it still works. That's exactly why it's safe to ask about personal documents you'd never paste into an online chatbot. (One note: if you connect the optional Telegram bot, those messages travel through Telegram like any chat — but the AI, your files, and the tools all stay on your Mac.)

Do I need to be technical?

Not at all. It's a normal menu-bar app: click to download a recommended model, then chat. The advanced bits — agent tools, coding assistants, the developer API — stay out of your way until you go looking for them.

What Mac do I need?

An Apple Silicon Mac — that's any M1, M2, M3, M4, or M5. Small, quick models run happily on 8–16GB of memory; more memory unlocks larger, smarter models and heavier things like video and 3D. The app recommends a model that fits your machine.

Which model should I pick?

Start with the recommended one — it balances speed and quality for your Mac. When you're curious, open Models and try a bigger one; smarter usually means a little slower. You can keep several and switch anytime.

Does it work offline?

Yes. You need internet once, to download a model. After that, chatting, image generation, voice, and the rest all work fully offline — on a plane, in a cabin, anywhere.

Will it slow my Mac down?

It uses the graphics chip while it's answering, then goes idle. You can unload the model when you're done to free up memory, and load it again in a click next time.
